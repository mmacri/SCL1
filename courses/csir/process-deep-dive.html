Process overview

Before, during, and after an incident

Preparation (before incidents)

  • Verify monitoring, backups, and contact lists
  • Review roles, responsibilities, and escalation paths
  • Practice response and evidence handling
  • Confirm reporting requirements and timeframes

Active Response (during incidents)

  • Detect and classify severity quickly
  • Contain safely while preserving evidence
  • Coordinate communications and approvals
  • Document actions and system state

Post-Incident (after resolution)

  • Recover operations and validate systems
  • Complete reporting and compliance follow-up
  • Capture lessons learned and update plans
  • Archive evidence and finalize logs

Severity level matrix

These severity levels align to the CSIR plan and drive when to escalate, involve compliance, and trigger DOE-417 / NERC-CIP reporting decisions.

Escalation decision tree

Observe an anomaly on OT systems?

Examples: alarms, unexpected reboots, unauthorized access attempts.

Is safety, reliability, or critical service at risk?
Yes
Escalate immediately: Incident Commander + ECS
Is the impact unclear or evidence limited?
Yes
Notify supervisor + ECS for triage
No impact and clearly benign?
No
Document and monitor; notify supervisor

When in doubt, treat events as at least Medium and notify ECS / SOC and your supervisor for triage; under-escalation is riskier than over-escalation.

Role responsibilities

Who does what in a CSIR event

Incident Commander

  • During containment: approve options balancing safety, reliability, and evidence preservation
  • During recovery: decide when to return systems to service and brief leadership
  • Confirm reportability and escalation timing

Technical Lead

  • During detection: validate alerts with ECS / SOC and assess operational risk
  • During containment: direct safe isolation actions
  • During recovery: confirm systems are restored, patched, and monitored

Communications Lead

  • During escalation: align internal updates and executive briefings
  • During response: coordinate PIO and external messaging
  • Ensure approved statements and consistent status updates

ECS / SOC

  • During detection: monitor alerts and triage incidents
  • During response: support evidence capture and containment
  • During follow-up: guide reporting timelines and documentation

Compliance Lead

  • During response: advise on reportability and DOE-417 drafts
  • During follow-up: ensure audit-ready evidence packages
  • Track regulatory obligations and deadlines

Operational impact and certification requirements
